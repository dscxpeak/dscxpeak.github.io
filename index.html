Challenges


Olist is an e-commerce marketplace integrator, connecting small businesses from all over Brazil to different customers. The businesses are able to sell their products through the Olist Store and ship them directly to the customers using Olist logistics partners. A wide range of products are sold by Olist, with customers having very different preferences (e.g. clothing vs flowers vs baths.

This is real commercial data from between 2016 and 2018. It contains 100k orders. It has been anonymized, and references to the companies and partners in the review text have been replaced with the names of Game of Thrones great houses. It is publicly available on Kaggle.

Exploring Customer Preferences: The Sandbox Challenge

Goal: To enhance the shopping experience of customers, retailers need to know as much about customer preferences and behaviour as possible

  • Data such as transactional, user, and product data can be used to generate insights about customers
  • These insights can enhance the experience of customers and make them more likely to purchase, increasing sales for the retailer
  • They can also allow retailers to explore demand for their different products and see if their current stock is performing well.
  • This may lead to a shift in direction for the business, through focusing on different products or trends in the future

During this challenge, you will generate insights for an online retailer based on the data available

  • You will present these insights back at the end of the datathon, imagining that you are a data scientist presenting to a retailer that has hired you to do this work
  • This challenge will require great technical and communication skills, with each group analysing the data and also explaining why your insights are beneficial to the retailer

Recommendation System: The Machine Learning Challenge

Goal: To enhance the shopping experience of customers, product recommenders are often used

  • They take transactional, user, and product data to provide personalised recommendations
  • These allow customers to be targeted with the products that they are most likely to purchase, increasing sales for the retailer

During this challenge, you will construct a product recommender

  • You will generate products that you think should be recommended to a list of customers based on the data available
  • You will then report your findings back, imagining that you are a data scientist presenting to a retailer that has hired you to do this work
  • This challenge will require great technical and communication skills, with each group constructing a recommender and also explaining what you have done to a non-technical audience

Judging Criteria

Peak is looking to judge your solution in the following aspects:

  • Technical Skills:
    • Showing that you have engaged with the challenge and the data
    • An analysis of the data and any potential issues with it
    • Critical thinking about your work and findings
    • Being able to come up with something to present
  • Communication/Presentation Skills
    • Working as part of a team to produce a great presentation
    • Your ability to present to a non-technical audience
    • Your ability to be clear and concise
    • Creating figures that are suitable for the audience and are easy to understand
